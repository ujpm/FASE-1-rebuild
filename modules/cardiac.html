Introduction to Cardiac Care

Cardiac emergencies are life-threatening situations that require immediate attention. Understanding how to recognize and respond to these emergencies can mean the difference between life and death.

Learning Objectives
  • Identify signs and symptoms of cardiac emergencies
  • Learn proper emergency response procedures
  • Understand prevention strategies
  • Practice decision-making in emergency scenarios

Signs & Symptoms

Common Warning Signs
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Nausea and cold sweats
  • Pain in arms, back, neck, or jaw
  • Lightheadedness or fainting

It's essential to note that symptoms can vary between individuals, and some people may experience atypical symptoms.

Emergency Response

Immediate Actions
  1. Call emergency services (911)
  2. Help the person into a comfortable position
  3. Loosen tight clothing
  4. Stay with the person and monitor their condition
  5. Be prepared to perform CPR if necessary

Prevention Strategies

Lifestyle Changes
  • Regular exercise
  • Healthy diet
  • Stress management
  • Regular check-ups
Risk Factors to Monitor
  • Blood pressure
  • Cholesterol levels
  • Blood sugar
  • Body weight
