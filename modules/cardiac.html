Heart Attack Response

Learn how to recognize and respond to heart attack emergencies using locally available resources.

Module Progress

Introduction to Heart Attacks

A heart attack occurs when blood flow to part of the heart muscle is blocked. Understanding the signs and responding quickly can save lives.

Recognizing Symptoms

Common Symptoms
  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Pain in arms, neck, jaw, or back
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold sweat
  • Nausea

Immediate Response Steps

Call your local emergency number immediately. If unavailable, arrange fast transportation to the nearest hospital.

Local Emergency Numbers:
  • General Emergency: 112
  • Ambulance: [Local Number]

Using Available Resources

Aspirin (if available)

If the person is not allergic to aspirin and it's readily available, they may chew one adult aspirin (325 mg) or four low-dose aspirins (81 mg).

Comfortable Position

Help the person find a comfortable semi-sitting position, supported by pillows or any firm cushioning available.
