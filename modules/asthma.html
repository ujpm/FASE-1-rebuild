Recognizing Symptoms

Important: Early recognition of asthma symptoms is crucial for effective response.
Wheezing

Whistling sound when breathing

Chest Tightness

Feeling of constriction

Coughing

Especially at night

Shortness of Breath

Difficulty breathing

Assessing Severity

Mild Attack
  • Minor wheezing
  • Can speak in sentences
  • Can walk normally
  • Normal alertness
Moderate Attack
  • Obvious wheezing
  • Speaks in phrases
  • Walking difficult
  • Using accessory muscles
Severe Attack
  • Severe wheezing
  • Single word speech
  • Hunched posture
  • Confusion possible
Emergency Warning Signs:
  • Blue lips or fingernails
  • Unable to speak or walk
  • Severe chest retractions
  • Drowsiness or confusion

Emergency Response

Inhaler Usage Guide

Prepare

Shake inhaler well

Position

Remove cap, hold upright

Breathe

Exhale fully first

Hold

Hold breath 10 seconds

Response Steps

  1. Stay Calm

    Help the person remain calm and seated upright.

  2. Use Inhaler

    Help them use their rescue inhaler as prescribed.

  3. Monitor

    Watch for improvement or worsening of symptoms.

  4. Get Help

    Call emergency services if symptoms are severe or not improving.

Prevention & Aftercare

Trigger Avoidance
  • Identify common triggers
  • Monitor air quality
  • Keep environment clean
  • Avoid known allergens
Action Plan
  • Have written plan ready
  • Keep inhaler accessible
  • Regular check-ups
  • Update medications
