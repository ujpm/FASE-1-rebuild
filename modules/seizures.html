Understanding Seizures

Important: Seizures are sudden, uncontrolled electrical disturbances in the brain.
Focal Seizures

Affect one part of the brain, may cause localized symptoms

Generalized Seizures

Involve both sides of the brain, causing more widespread symptoms

Absence Seizures

Brief lapses of consciousness, often in children

Tonic-Clonic

Most dramatic type, involving loss of consciousness and body stiffening

Immediate Seizure Response

Stay Calm: Your composed response can prevent further injury.
1. Protect from Injury

Move nearby objects away, create a safe space around the person

2. Do Not Restrain

Never try to stop the person's movements or hold them down

3. Time the Seizure

Note the duration to inform medical professionals

4. Turn to Side

If possible, gently turn the person to their side to prevent choking

First Aid for Seizures

Remember: Most seizures end on their own within 2-3 minutes.
When to Call Emergency Services
  • Seizure lasts longer than 5 minutes
  • Person doesn't regain consciousness
  • Seizure occurs in water
  • Person is pregnant or has diabetes
  • Injury occurs during the seizure
After the Seizure
  • Stay with the person until fully awake
  • Speak calmly and reassuringly
  • Help them sit in a safe, comfortable position
  • Check for any injuries
  • Offer water when they're fully alert

Seizure Prevention

Proactive Approach: Many seizures can be managed or prevented.
Medical Management
  • Take prescribed anti-epileptic medications
  • Regular medical check-ups
  • Manage underlying health conditions
Lifestyle Strategies
  • Get adequate sleep
  • Manage stress
  • Avoid known triggers
  • Maintain a healthy diet
  • Limit alcohol consumption
