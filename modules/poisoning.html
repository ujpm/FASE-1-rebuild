Understanding Poisoning

Definition: Poisoning occurs when a toxic substance is ingested, inhaled, injected, or absorbed.
Medication Overdose

Accidental or intentional consumption of excessive medication

Chemical Exposure

Contact with harmful industrial or household chemicals

Alcohol Poisoning

Excessive alcohol consumption leading to toxic effects

Biological Toxins

Poisoning from plants, insects, or animal bites

Recognizing Poisoning Symptoms

Warning: Symptoms can vary widely depending on the type of poison.
Common Physical Symptoms
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Confusion or disorientation
  • Excessive sweating
  • Seizures
Potential Behavioral Signs
  • Unusual drowsiness
  • Aggressive or erratic behavior
  • Loss of coordination
  • Unexplained burns or rashes
  • Unusual breath odor

Immediate Poisoning Response

Critical Steps: Quick and correct action can save lives.
1. Ensure Safety

Remove person from exposure source, protect yourself from contamination

2. Call Poison Control

Contact local poison control center (1-800-222-1222 in the US)

3. Gather Information

Collect poison container, estimate amount and time of exposure

4. Monitor Symptoms

Watch for breathing difficulties, consciousness changes

5. Follow Professional Advice

Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed by medical professionals

Specific Poison Response

Caution: Different poisons require different approaches.
Medication Overdose
  • Call emergency services immediately
  • Provide medication bottle to responders
  • Do not give anything by mouth
  • Keep person warm and calm
Chemical Exposure
  • Remove contaminated clothing
  • Flush skin with cool water for 15 minutes
  • Avoid spreading chemical
  • Seek medical attention
Alcohol Poisoning
  • Call emergency services
  • Keep person awake if possible
  • Prevent choking
  • Monitor breathing

Poison Prevention Strategies

Proactive Approach: Prevention is the best protection.
Home Safety
  • Lock up medications and chemicals
  • Use child-proof containers
  • Store chemicals in original containers
  • Keep emergency numbers visible
Personal Precautions
  • Read labels carefully
  • Wear protective gear when handling chemicals
  • Never mix cleaning products
  • Educate children about dangers
